## 2. Proposal Structure

### Part B1 (Evaluated at Step 1)
**Cover Page** — PI name, host institution, title, acronym, duration, abstract (half page)
- For cross-panel proposals: indicate additional panel(s) and justify

**Part I of the Scientific Proposal** (max **5 pages**, references excluded)
- Must contain ALL essential information — Step 1 evaluators only see this + CV
- Content (any order/format):
  1. Current state of knowledge
  2. Scientific question and objectives
  3. Overall approach / research strategy
  4. Contribution to the field — what will be changed/opened/challenged

**Curriculum Vitae and Track Record** (max **4 pages**)
- Personal details, education, key qualifications
- Current and relevant previous positions
- PhD supervisor(s) and postdoctoral mentor(s)
- Up to 10 research outputs with significance explanations
- Selected examples of significant peer recognition
- Career breaks, diverse career paths, life events (optional)
- Noteworthy community contributions beyond research (optional)

### Part B2 (Evaluated at Step 2 only)
**Part II of the Scientific Proposal** (max **7 pages**, references excluded)
- Detailed project implementation
- Research methodology
- Work plan
- Risk assessment and mitigating measures
- NOT a repetition of Part I
- Budget justification should be in Part A Section 3, but B2 can add methodology-related justification

**Funding ID** (does not count towards page limits)
- Current research grants and their subjects
- Ongoing/submitted applications related to the proposal

## 3. Evaluation Criteria

### Step 1 (B1 only — Part I + CV):
**Research Project (ground-breaking nature & ambition):**
- Does the research address **important scientific questions**?
- Are the objectives **ambitious** and will it **advance the frontier of knowledge**?

**Principal Investigator (intellectual capacity & creativity):**
- Has the PI demonstrated ability to conduct **groundbreaking research**?
- Does the PI provide evidence of **creative and original thinking**?
- Does the PI have the required **scientific expertise and capacity** to execute?

### Step 2 (B1 + B2 + Budget):
All Step 1 criteria **PLUS**:
- Is the **research methodology** appropriate?
- Are **timescales and resources** adequate and properly justified?

### Key Insights from Enspire & Evaluation Docs:
- **Scientific excellence is the SOLE criterion**
- Step 1 evaluators act as **generalists** — write for broad CS audience
- Up to **44 proposals per panel** retained for Step 2
- Must be **non-incremental** — not just a continuation of existing work
- Must show **high-risk/high-gain** character
- Avoid "fishing expeditions" — must have clear hypotheses
- Don't fragment the project — maintain a coherent narrative
- ERC is **open-ended** — results should open new research directions

## 4. Structural Analysis: WoMbAM as Reference

The successful WoMbAM ERC StG proposal (Stöber, Göttingen) provides an excellent PE6-adjacent template:

### B1 Structure (WoMbAM):
1. **Cover Page**: PI, institution, duration, extended abstract (half page)
2. **Ground-breaking Nature & Impact** — compelling vision statement
3. **State-of-the-art & Open Questions** — identifies specific gap
4. **Objectives** (3 objectives, each with 3 WPs = 9 WPs total)
   - Obj 1: Understand (diagnostic/analytical)
   - Obj 2: Build (engineering/novel architecture)
   - Obj 3: Validate (against biological data)
5. **Qualification & interdisciplinary approach** — PI expertise + collaborators
6. **Major Outcomes** — science, technology, society impacts
7. **CV and Track Record** — publications, positions, funding, awards

### B2 Structure (WoMbAM):
1. **Research methodology** — detailed WP descriptions (Problem → Approach → Evaluation → Contribution for each)
2. **Work and time schedule** — Gantt chart with milestones
3. **Feasibility, Risks & Contingency Plans**
4. **Collaborations**
5. **Integration into Host Institute**
6. **Recruitment measures**
7. **Ethics**
8. **Funding ID** (appendix, not counted)

### Key WoMbAM Patterns to Adopt:
- 3 Objectives with clear "Understand → Build → Validate" arc
- Each WP follows: Problem → Approach → Evaluation → Contribution
- Preliminary results shown with figures
- Collaborators justified with tangible history (co-authored papers, visits)
- Team: PI + 2 PhDs (compact, focused)
- Clear NeuroAI framing with bidirectional loop
- Cross-panel justification (when applicable)
